    FAQs

    1. What is Solid Waste Management?
    - Solid waste management involves the collection, transportation, processing, recycling, and disposal of solid waste materials generated by human activities. It aims to minimize the adverse effects of waste on public health, the environment, and aesthetics.
    2. Why is solid waste management important?
    - Effective solid waste management is crucial for protecting public health, preventing pollution, conserving resources, and promoting sustainable development. Proper waste management reduces the risk of disease transmission, minimizes environmental contamination, and contributes to the efficient use of resources.
    3.What are the different types of solid waste?
    - Solid waste can be categorized into various types, including municipal solid waste (MSW), industrial waste, hazardous waste, construction and demolition debris, agricultural waste, and electronic waste (e-waste). Each type of waste may require specific handling and disposal methods.
